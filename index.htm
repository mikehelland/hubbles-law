Hubble's Universe

According to legend, Edwin Hubble discovered that the universe is expanding. In reality, he never accepted that interpretation of the observations:

The familiar interpretation of red-shifts as velocity-shifts very seriously restricts not only the time scale, the age of the universe, but the spatial dimensions as well. On the other hand, the alternative possible interpretation, that red-shifts are not velocity-shifts, avoids both difficulties, and presents the observable region as an insignificant sample of a universe that extends indefinitely in space and in time.

E. Hubble, The Observational Approach to Cosmology, pg. 47, 1937

What follows is an irreverant examination of fundamental cosmology, hopefully one that Hubble would approve of.

An alternative quantification of redshift

Cosmological redshifts are fundamental to the study of the universe at large scales. It is shown that by inverting the traditional equations of redshift, the infinite range of quantifiable redshifts becomes finite. This implies an alternative redshift-distance relationship that correlates closely to the predictions of the concordance model of cosmology.
