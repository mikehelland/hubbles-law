The universe today has carbon, oxygen, lead, etc

But before the first stars, only hydrogen and helium existed

Anything besides H and He are called "metals"

      =       "metals"                  

Initially the universe didn't have any metal


Then stars formed, fused H and He into heavier atoms, and blew up.

And the universe got metal!


(And ozzy was happy)

Origins of this idea go back to the 1940's and 1950's

How does it hold up to modern measurements?


  • The most distant measure of the ratio of oxygen / hydrogen is at $z=12.6$
  • Compare to the sun's current value ("solar")
  • These are all metal poor galaxies
  • The highest metallicity at the highest redshift is observed at $z=4.2$

Notes:

  • These studies targeted different objects with different instruments
  • They used different ways to measure oxygen abundance
  • Other metals (C, Ne, Fe, S, etc.) count toward metallicity too
  • This mash-up of data isn't conclusive evidence of anything
  • A different paper estimates GS-z12's O/H as 6.6, not 7.9
