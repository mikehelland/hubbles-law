Redshift review:

  • $\lambda$ is wavelength
  • $f$ is frequency

$1+z = \frac{\lambda_{obs}}{\lambda_{emit}} = \frac{f_{emit}}{f_{obs}}$


For $z=1$, wavelength appears double, frequency appears half

$1+z = \frac{2}{1} = \frac{1}{0.5}$

Let's put a light clock at $z=1$

light clock


  • Light source and detecor
  • Mirror ½ light-second away
  • ~150,000 km
  • A light pulse is sent
  • When the pulse comes back, one second has passed
  • The process repeats

  • And an additional pulse is sent into space

  • We're at $z=0$, in the present
  • Light clock at $z=1$, in the past
  • Pulses are sent 1 second apart on the past clock
  • We observe the past clock to run at ½ speed
  • Our clock runs at 2x speed the past clock
  • Our clock counts 2 seconds between pulses
  • An observer in the past would still count 1 second between pulses
  • That observer would still measure the speed of light to be $c$.
  • According to our clock, there are 2 seconds between pulses
  • According to our clock, light is traveling at $c/2$ at $z=1$

Therefore:

If an observer's clock is running at $\frac{1}{1+z}$ speed...

... then to be measured as $c$ on that clock ...

... light has to be moving at $\frac{c}{1+z}$ there.

  • At $z=1$, light is moving at $c/2$
  • At $z=0$, light is moving at $c$
  • Light traveling from $z=1$ to $z=0$ moves at all speeds in between


$0.1 \frac{c}{2.0} + 0.1 \frac{c}{1.9} + 0.1 \frac{c}{1.8} + ... + 0.1 \frac{c}{1.0} $
